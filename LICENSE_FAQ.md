## Detailed FAQ

### General Questions

#### 1. What is dual licensing?

SynQ is available under two licenses:

1. **MIT License** - Open source, free for everyone
2. **Commercial License** - Paid license for commercial use

You choose which license applies to your use case.

#### 2. Why does SynQ use dual licensing?

Dual licensing allows us to:
- Build a strong open-source community
- Support commercial users who need enterprise features
- Sustain development through commercial revenue
- Maintain open-source principles

#### 3. Can I use SynQ without paying?

Yes! The MIT License is completely free. Use it for:
- Personal projects
- Educational purposes
- Open-source projects
- Non-profit organizations
- Academic research

#### 4. When do I need to pay?

You need a Commercial License when using SynQ for:
- Commercial products or services
- Enterprise deployments
- SaaS offerings
- Revenue-generating activities
- Proprietary research

### MIT License Questions

#### 5. What can I do with the MIT License?

With the MIT License, you can:
- ✅ Use SynQ for any purpose
- ✅ Modify SynQ
- ✅ Distribute SynQ
- ✅ Use SynQ in commercial products
- ✅ Use SynQ in SaaS services
- ✅ Sublicense SynQ

**Requirements:**
- ❗ Include the MIT License text
- ❗ Include copyright notice
- ❗ Provide attribution to SynQ

#### 6. Can I use MIT License for commercial products?

Yes! The MIT License permits commercial use. However:
- You must include the MIT License text
- You must provide attribution
- You must include copyright notice
- We recommend getting a Commercial License for enterprise support

#### 7. Do I need to open-source my project if I use MIT License?

No! The MIT License does NOT require you to open-source your project. You can use SynQ in proprietary products under the MIT License.

#### 8. What must I include if I use MIT License?

You must include:
1. The full MIT License text
2. Copyright notice: "Copyright (c) 2026 TangoSplicer"
3. Attribution to SynQ in documentation or about page

Example:
```
This product includes SynQ, which is licensed under the MIT License.
Copyright (c) 2026 TangoSplicer
```

#### 9. Can I modify SynQ under MIT License?

Yes! You can:
- Modify the source code
- Create derivative works
- Distribute modifications

**Requirements:**
- Include the MIT License
- Include copyright notice
- Provide attribution

#### 10. Can I distribute SynQ under MIT License?

Yes! You can distribute SynQ as long as you:
- Include the MIT License text
- Include copyright notice
- Don't remove attribution

The Commercial License is a paid license that provides:
- Right to use SynQ for commercial purposes

#### 12. When do I need a Commercial License?

You need a Commercial License if you:
- Sell products or services using SynQ
- Provide SaaS using SynQ
- Deploy SynQ in enterprise environments
- Use SynQ for revenue-generating activities
- Want priority support
